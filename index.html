About Me

I am Bomin, a 2nd year Computer Science and Biology student at University of Toronto Scarborough. My passion lies at bioinformatics, which is the intersection of these two disciplines. I enjoy learning different data structures, simulating molecular dynamics, coding pipelines, and thinking about abstract math concepts.

Outside of my studies, I have a few other interests. I find it fascinating to read about new developments in science and technology, which expand my scientific imagination. My main interests include -omics, microbiomes, and AI/ML, though I occasionally get drawn into astrophysics as well. Additionally, I love playing badminton. My goal is to master a powerful smash and improve my defensive skills.

Projects

RNA-seq Pipeline

Constructed a data preprocssing pipeline for RNA-seq analysis using linux shell scripting language. This pipeline lets us promptly obtain the counts file from FASTQ file with quality control. The counts file generated is useful for RNA-seq studies such as differential gene expression analysis.

Toronto Asian Arts Museum App

Developed an app for the Toronto Asian Arts Museum (TAAM) featuring the collections for customers and allowing effortless data management for administrators, as part of the project for course CSCB07. Easy-to-use interface targets users with a wide range of digital literacy.

CSCB20 Course Website

Designed a course website that allows the students and the instructors to communicate in one place, as a part of the project for course CSCB20. This website provides the students with comprehensive access point to the course materials and offers straightforward grade management for the instructors.
